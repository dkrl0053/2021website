We are riders—that fact has guided our every decision since 1974.

When people wanted to ride cruisers in the dirt,

When roadies wanted to go faster, we doubled-down on carbon

we started the Specialized Foundation to help them through cycling.

BECAUSE MOUNTAIN
BIKES NEED TRAILS

As quickly as the mountain bike arrived,
so did the issue of trail access. By 1987,
bikes were getting the boot from public lands.
That year, we took part in a round table of
advocacy groups, and within six months,
the International Mountain Bike Association (IMBA)
was born. In short order, we became trail access evangelists.
We sponsored the first industry-wide meeting on the subject,
and some of our employees went to sit on IMBA’s board.
Without trail advocates, there’d be no trails to protect.
It’s as simple as that.
